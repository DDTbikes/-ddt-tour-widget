Itinerary

Day 1: Galbraith Mountain
Morning

Your guided mountain bike trip kicks off with a morning meet-and-greet at our downtown Bellingham hotel. From there, we load up and head to the south side of Galbraith Mountain — 3,000 acres and 100+ miles of singletrack right in our backyard, and one of the premier mountain biking destinations in Washington State. We'll start with a warm-up climb through towering second-growth forest, getting our legs dialed and taking in views of Bellingham Bay peeking through the trees.

Your guide will tailor the morning ride to the group — beginners will enjoy smooth flow trails with gentle gradients, while more experienced riders can tackle optional features and technical singletrack sections. By mid-morning we'll have found our rhythm and earned a well-deserved trailside lunch.

Afternoon

Afternoon is where the magic really happens. We'll work our way up to some of Galbraith's signature descents — think luscious flow, perfect berms, and that meditative feeling of linking turn after turn through Pacific Northwest rainforest. The trails here reward every level of rider, and by the time we roll back to the trailhead, the crew's spirits will be sky-high.

We'll wrap up the day with a cold beverage at one of Bellingham's legendary craft breweries before settling in for a locally-sourced dinner downtown — all included in your mountain bike tour package.

12–16 mi  ·  ~2,000–2,500' elev.
Day 2: Chuckanut & Larrabee
Morning

Day two of our multi-day mountain bike tour takes us south along the iconic Chuckanut Drive to Larrabee State Park — Washington's very first state park, and home to some of the best singletrack descents in the Pacific Northwest. We'll climb through old-growth rainforest with postcard views of the San Juan Islands below.

For the all-levels group, we'll ride a mix of scenic cross-country trails and mellow singletrack through towering cedars. Advanced riders have the option of a shuttle-assisted descent — a ripping 1,600-foot drop on trails that blend flow and tech through ancient forest. This is the kind of mountain biking Washington State is famous for.

Afternoon

After a picnic lunch with ocean views, we'll head to the Chuckanut ridge trails for an afternoon session. The trail network here offers a completely different feel from Galbraith — more rooted, more raw, and with a wild coastal rainforest atmosphere that's uniquely PNW.

Back in town, we'll hit up a local favorite for dinner and share stories from the day's mountain bike adventure. Undoubtedly tired and satisfied, we'll rest up for our final day.

10–14 mi  ·  ~1,500–2,200' elev.
Day 3: Blanchard Mountain

The final day of our guided mountain bike trip features a backcountry adventure on Blanchard Mountain, a 4,500-acre state forest about 20 minutes south of town. This is where the wild side of PNW mountain biking really shines. We'll climb through dense rainforest to reach ridgeline singletrack with sweeping views of Samish Bay and the San Juans.

The descent rewards the climb — flowing singletrack through mossy old-growth forest, with options ranging from smooth intermediate flow trails to more technical terrain for those who want it. This is the kind of mountain bike adventure where you forget about everything else and just find the flow state.

Advanced riders: For those with energy to spare, we offer an optional shuttle day at Darrington — a rugged backcountry mountain biking experience with big descents and remote alpine terrain.

We'll finish the tour with a celebratory lunch back in Bellingham, basking in some post-ride glow before saying our goodbyes. Three days of fully guided singletrack, world-class trails, and memories for a lifetime — that's the Bellingham mountain bike experience.

10–15 mi  ·  ~1,800–2,500' elev.
